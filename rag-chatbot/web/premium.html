What is RAG?

Understanding Retrieval-Augmented Generation

The Concept

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a powerful AI technique that combines the best of both worlds: the vast knowledge of large language models and the specific, up-to-date information from your documents.

Why RAG Matters

Traditional AI models are trained on data up to a certain point in time and can't access your private documents. RAG solves this by retrieving relevant information from your documents and using it to generate accurate, contextual answers.

How It Works

1
Document Processing

Your documents are broken down into smaller chunks and converted into mathematical representations (vectors) that capture their meaning.

2
Query Understanding

When you ask a question, the system converts your query into the same vector format to find matching content.

3
Retrieval Process

The system searches through your documents to find the most relevant chunks that match your question using both keyword and semantic search.

4
Answer Generation

The AI uses the retrieved information as context to generate accurate, well-sourced answers with proper citations.

Key Benefits

  • Accuracy: Answers are grounded in your actual documents, not just general AI knowledge
  • Transparency: Every answer includes citations showing exactly which documents were used
  • Up-to-date: Information is as current as your most recently uploaded document
  • Privacy: Your documents stay within your system - no external data sharing
  • Relevance: Responses are tailored to your specific content and context

How to Use RAG Assistant

Your complete guide to getting started

Step 1: Upload Your Documents

Start by adding the documents you want to analyze. Our system supports various file formats:

Supported File Types

  • PDF: Research papers, reports, manuals
  • TXT: Plain text files, notes, transcripts
  • MD: Markdown files, documentation
  • DOCX: Word documents, proposals
A
Drag & Drop

Simply drag files from your computer onto the chat interface

B
Upload Button

Click the paperclip icon in the chat to browse and select files

Step 2: Start Asking Questions

Once your documents are uploaded, you can ask natural language questions. Here are some effective question types:

Effective Question Examples

  • "What are the main conclusions of this research?"
  • "Summarize the key findings about [specific topic]"
  • "What methodology was used in this study?"
  • "Compare the results from different sections"
  • "What are the statistics mentioned about [topic]?"

Step 3: Understanding Citations

Every answer includes citations that show you exactly where the information came from:

  • Source Files: See which document contained the information
  • Relevance Scores: Understand how closely each source matches your question
  • Content Previews: Quick snippets of the relevant text
  • Full Context: Click citations to see the complete context

Step 4: Customize Your Experience

Use the Settings panel to optimize your experience:

1
Retrieval Settings

Adjust how many document chunks to retrieve and how to combine search results

2
Interface Settings

Switch between light and dark modes, enable/disable streaming responses

3
Performance Monitoring

View real-time statistics about response times and system performance

Pro Tips

  • Be Specific: More detailed questions often yield better results
  • Use Keywords: Include important terms from your documents
  • Follow Up: Ask clarifying questions to dig deeper
  • Check Citations: Always review sources to verify information
  • Experiment: Try different phrasings if you don't get the answer you need
